What flowers are in season in Los Angeles?

A month-by-month look at what's fresh, what's at its peak, and what's grown right here in California — so you can plan an event around the good stuff or just know what's best this week.

Southern California's mild climate means something is always in bloom, and the LA Flower Market carries a huge range year-round. But freshness and price still follow the seasons. Pick a month below to see what local growers are cutting — Peak season flags California blooms at their absolute best right now.

Why buy in season?

Fresher and longer-lasting

Seasonal, locally grown stems spend days getting to your vase — not weeks in a shipping container. They open better and last noticeably longer.

Better prices

When a flower is at its peak, supply is high and cost comes down. Building an arrangement around what's in season is the easiest way to get more blooms for your budget.

Supports California flower farms

California grows the majority of America's cut flowers. Choosing in-season, Certified American Grown stems keeps those local farms — and the LA Flower Market they supply — thriving.
